The Program

An AI degree built for the rest of us.

A 60-unit Associate of Science for learners without a computer science background. Six stackable courses teach you to use, evaluate, and deploy AI through no-code and low-code tools — with ethics, human oversight, and workforce outcomes at the center.

01

No-code / low-code first

Build real AI workflows without writing traditional code. We focus on the tools industry actually uses — ChatGPT, Claude, Zapier, Make, Voiceflow, Power Automate, Hugging Face Spaces.

02

Ethics-first framing

Bias, privacy, transparency, and human oversight are threaded through every course — not bolted on at the end. You'll learn to question every AI output, not just generate them.

03

Stackable & transferable

Earn certificates along the way. All six courses transfer to both UC and CSU. Finish with an A.S. degree and a portfolio of real AI projects you can show employers.

The Pathway

Six courses. One clear path.

Begin with foundations, branch through applied specializations, finish with a capstone where you ship something real. Each course is three units and can be taken as a standalone certificate — or stacked into the full A.S. degree.

Who It's For

Built for everyone the AI wave is reshaping.

Which, at this point, is basically everyone. If you work with information, make decisions, talk to customers, manage workflows, or create anything — AI is in your job description now.

01 · Career Changers

Pivoting into tech without a CS degree

You want to move into AI work without first spending four years learning to code. The certificates stack toward a degree — and you'll leave with a portfolio.

02 · Working Professionals

Upskilling to stay ahead of the shift

Your job is changing. You want to be the person who uses AI well, not the person replaced by someone who does. Evening and flexible scheduling supports working adults.

03 · Small Business & Nonprofits

Automating the work you can't hire for

You run lean. You need AI to handle the marketing copy, customer intake, or reporting that's eating your week. Learn the no-code tools and ship workflows by semester's end.

04 · Recent Graduates

Adding AI fluency to any résumé

You just finished a bachelor's in something else. You need the AI literacy employers now expect — with artifacts you can point to, not just buzzwords.

05 · Educators & Public Sector

Using AI in public-interest work

You work in schools, government, or community services. You need the ethics-first framing, the accessibility emphasis, and practical deployment skills for AI in public life.

06 · The AI-Curious

You've been lurking. Come do the work.

You've been watching ChatGPT and the AI conversation from the outside. You want a structured path — and actual hands on the tools. Start with the foundations course.

Jobs this degree opens up

You won't graduate as a machine learning engineer — that's a different program. You'll graduate ready for the AI-adjacent roles that every industry is hiring right now:

AI Operations Specialist

Deploy and monitor AI workflows inside an organization. Keep systems running responsibly.

Prompt Engineer

Design, test, and refine the inputs that make AI systems useful for specific business tasks.

Automation Analyst

Map workflows and build multi-step automations with Zapier, Make, or Power Automate.

Conversational AI Designer

Design chatbot and voice-assistant flows for customer service, healthcare, and education.

AI Content Strategist

Combine generative AI fluency with editorial judgment for marketing, comms, and education.

AI Ethics & Accessibility Reviewer

Audit AI systems for bias, privacy risk, and inclusive design — a rising compliance role.

Digital Transformation Analyst

Help small and mid-sized organizations actually adopt AI without wrecking what works.
