Financial Aid

There's almost always a way to pay for this.

Long Beach City College offers multiple aid pathways. Most eligible students pay substantially less than the sticker price — often nothing at all.

01 · California College Promise

Free first two years

First-time, full-time California residents can attend LBCC tuition-free for up to two years through the California College Promise Grant.

02 · FAFSA / Pell Grant

Federal need-based aid

Submit the FAFSA (Free Application for Federal Student Aid). If eligible, Pell Grants can cover tuition plus books, supplies, and living costs.

03 · CA Dream Act

Aid for AB 540 students

Undocumented and AB 540 students can apply for state aid through the California Dream Act — separate from FAFSA, same level of support.

04 · CTE / Perkins Funding

Career-track specific aid

Because this is a Career Technical Education program, additional Perkins-funded support may be available for books, materials, and childcare.

05 · Veterans Benefits

GI Bill & state veteran fee waiver

LBCC is approved for VA education benefits. California residents who served may also qualify for the CalVet College Fee Waiver.

06 · Employer Tuition Assistance

If your employer pays for learning

Many employers reimburse tuition for relevant professional development. This program counts as workforce upskilling — ask your HR team.

Frequently Asked

Questions, answered.

Do I need any coding background to start?

No. This program is explicitly designed for learners without a computer science background. The entire curriculum uses no-code and low-code tools — meaning you'll build real AI workflows through visual interfaces and prompts rather than writing traditional programming code.

If you can use a web browser and a spreadsheet, you can succeed in COSA 55 / AI 40. The later courses build gradually; you're never thrown into the deep end.

Do I need a math background?

The program does not require calculus, linear algebra, or statistics beyond high-school level. You'll encounter concepts like "how a model evaluates accuracy" or "what algorithmic bias looks like in practice" — but the focus is on applied use, not building models from math first principles.

How long does the full A.S. degree take?

Two years full-time, longer part-time. The six AI courses (18 units total) are the program core. The remaining 42 units are general education requirements — math, English, social sciences, etc. — that you can take alongside the AI courses or transfer in from previous college work.

If you only want AI skills without the full degree, you can take any single course as a standalone certificate.

Can I take just one course?

Yes. Every course is offered as a standalone. You don't need to commit to the full degree to enroll. Many students take COSA 55 / AI 40 as a single-semester skill-builder and decide from there.

Are classes in person, online, or hybrid?

Both formats are offered, depending on the term. Most courses have an in-person section at the LAC or PCC campus and a fully asynchronous online section. Check the class schedule for the specific term you're enrolling in.

Will these courses transfer to a four-year university?

Yes — all six courses in the program are transferable to both the UC and CSU systems. That means credits you earn here can apply toward a bachelor's degree at any UC or CSU campus, depending on the receiving institution's articulation agreements.

If you're planning to transfer, meet with an LBCC counselor early to map which UC or CSU programs best accept these credits toward a bachelor's.

What AI tools will I actually use?

The stack evolves with the industry, but the current core includes ChatGPT, Claude, Voiceflow, Zapier, Make, Microsoft Power Automate, Hugging Face Spaces, and no-code platforms like Google Teachable Machine. Specialized courses add image and audio tools (Midjourney, ElevenLabs, Whisper, Runway).

You'll use free and trial-tier access for most assignments — paid subscriptions aren't required.

I work full-time. Can I do this part-time?

Yes. Many students take one or two courses per term while working. Evening sections are typically available, and online sections are asynchronous — meaning you complete weekly work on your own schedule rather than attending live class sessions.

What if I'm an international student?

LBCC welcomes international students on F-1 visas. The International Student Office handles SEVIS, health insurance, and academic support. Tuition is higher for non-residents, so plan accordingly. Contact the International Student Office through lbcc.edu for application guidance.

Who teaches the AI courses?

The AI for Digital Transformation program is led by Prof. Vincent Calip, a faculty member in the Computer & Office Studies Department with a background in applied AI, instructional design, and workforce development. Prof. Calip is the faculty author for five of the six core courses.

Something else? Ask.

If your question isn't covered here, email vcalip@lbcc.edu. Prof. Calip responds to program inquiries directly — no gatekeeping, no form letter.
