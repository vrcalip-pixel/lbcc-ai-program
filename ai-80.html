Course Description

Applied AI Integration Studio

This advanced, project-based capstone course allows students to design, develop, and present an integrated AI solution that demonstrates comprehensive mastery of generative, conversational, and multi-modal AI systems. Students work in teams or individually to analyze a real-world problem, develop an applied AI workflow, and deploy a functional prototype using no-code and low-code tools. Emphasis is placed on ethical design, human–AI collaboration, system integration, and the presentation of professional documentation and analytics. The course culminates in a final portfolio and presentation showcasing a working AI solution for workforce or community impact.

Tools you'll work with: Stack of your choice across the program · Professional portfolio platforms (Notion, Framer, GitHub Pages) · Analytics and evaluation frameworks

What You'll Learn

By the end, you'll be able to —

  1. Define a project problem statement and scope aligned with workforce or community needs.
  2. Select and integrate appropriate AI tools and workflows across multiple modalities.
  3. Apply prompt engineering, data management, and automation techniques effectively.
  4. Demonstrate ethical reasoning by incorporating fairness, accountability, and transparency principles in design.
  5. Test, evaluate, and iterate prototypes based on performance data and stakeholder feedback.
  6. Document and present project outcomes through written, visual, and oral communication using professional standards.

What You'll Study

Course content, 54 lecture hours.

Topics, pacing, and activities pulled directly from the approved Course Outline of Record.

I

Orientation and Capstone Overview

  • Course expectations and assessment overview
  • Review of AI systems from COSA 55–69
  • Project planning, milestones, deliverables
3 hours
II

Problem Identification and Proposal

  • Identifying an authentic, real-world problem
  • User and stakeholder needs analysis
  • Proposal with goals, outcomes, evaluation criteria
  • Peer and instructor feedback
6 hours
III

System Architecture and Design

  • Selecting generative, conversational, multi-modal tools
  • Workflow design with no-code/low-code platforms
  • Data pipelines and automation logic
  • Ethical, inclusive, accessibility-centered design
9 hours
IV

Prototype Development and Integration

  • Building multi-modal AI prototypes
  • Integrating APIs, agents, automation workflows
  • Functional and usability testing
  • Iterative debugging and refinement
12 hours
V

Evaluation and Performance Analytics

  • Metrics for accuracy, reliability, usability
  • Ethical impact, transparency, human oversight
  • Reflective analysis of human–AI collaboration
8 hours
VI

Professional Presentation and Portfolio

  • Documentation, technical reports, artifacts
  • Visual presentations and demonstrations
  • Final oral presentation
  • Compiling the AI portfolio
10 hours
VII

Reflection and Future Directions

  • Post-project reflection on process and outcomes
  • Next-generation AI and workforce trends
  • Pathways for lifelong learning
6 hours
