Course Description

Applied Multi-Modal AI

This course introduces students to multi-modal artificial intelligence (AI), focusing on applied systems that integrate text, images, audio, and video for real-world tasks. Using no-code and low-code tools, students will design, test, and deploy multi-modal prototypes such as image captioning tools, cross-modal search, and voice-enabled assistants. Emphasis is placed on workforce applications, ethical design, and human-centered innovation. Designed for learners pursuing stackable AI certificates or foundational skills for careers in digital transformation, this course culminates in a portfolio-ready applied project.

Tools you'll work with: Hugging Face Spaces · Google Teachable Machine · OpenAI APIs · Whisper · ElevenLabs · Runway ML

What You'll Learn

By the end, you'll be able to —

  1. Define multi-modal AI and distinguish it from unimodal systems.
  2. Identify major applications of multi-modal AI across accessibility, healthcare, business operations, and creative industries.
  3. Demonstrate workflows such as speech-to-text, image classification, video summarization, and cross-modal retrieval.
  4. Apply no-code and low-code platforms — Hugging Face Spaces, Google Teachable Machine, OpenAI APIs — to build multi-modal solutions.
  5. Test prototypes with sample data and analyze outcomes for inclusivity and fairness.
  6. Critically assess ethical challenges such as surveillance, deepfakes, and algorithmic bias.
  7. Create and present a final project demonstrating a real-world applied multi-modal AI use case.

What You'll Study

Course content, 54 lecture hours.

Topics, pacing, and activities pulled directly from the approved Course Outline of Record.

I

Introduction to Multi-Modal AI

  • History and evolution from computer vision to integrated AI
  • Industry use cases: healthcare imaging, accessibility, creative
5 hours
II

Core Concepts

  • Representations of text, image, audio, video
  • Vision-language models, embeddings, cross-modal alignment
  • Generative vs. discriminative approaches
8 hours
III

Designing Multi-Modal Workflows

  • Combining inputs (image + text queries)
  • Creating outputs (captioning, video summarization)
  • Accessibility and inclusivity in design
7 hours
IV

Ethics and Social Implications

  • Privacy and surveillance in visual/audio AI
  • Bias in facial recognition, language, datasets
  • Responsible use in education, healthcare, business
6 hours
V

Hands-On Labs

  • Building image classification prototypes
  • Speech-to-text workflows
  • Text-to-image and multi-modal retrieval
10 hours
VI

Deployment and Integration

  • No-code/low-code deployment options
  • Integration with productivity apps
  • Accessibility testing and UDL
6 hours
VII

Testing and Analytics

  • Usability testing for multi-modal systems
  • Evaluating outputs for accuracy and inclusivity
  • Iterative refinement
6 hours
VIII

Capstone Project

  • Applied multi-modal prototype
  • Project showcase and portfolio documentation
6 hours
